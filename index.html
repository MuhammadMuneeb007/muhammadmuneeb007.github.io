About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computational Biology at The University of Queensland, working under the supervision of Dr. David Ascher. My research focuses on developing innovative computational methods for genomic analysis and leveraging artificial intelligence to advance bioinformatics research.

With a strong foundation in computer science and a passion for biological data analysis, I bridge the gap between computational methods and biological insights. My work encompasses automating systematic literature reviews using natural language processing, benchmarking polygenic risk score tools, and developing machine learning models for genotype-phenotype predictions.
